# Company Setup

This is the practical legal-and-operations checklist for launching under the
current constraints.

This is not legal or tax advice.

## Cleanest $0 launch path

Because Oregon assumed-business-name filings cost money, the cleanest exact-$0
launch path is usually:

- sell as a sole proprietor under your real legal name first
- keep the product name as a product name, not the formal seller name
- fund a DBA later from revenue if you still want one

Current direction:

- no LLC for launch
- sole proprietor only unless the plan changes later
- if the seller-facing name is not the legal name, re-check Oregon assumed
  business name requirements before taking paid orders

## Before taking money

- decide the public seller identity and record it in writing
- create the business email you will actually monitor
- decide whether to obtain a free EIN now
- check Oregon Business Xpress and the Oregon license directory for any city or
  county requirements tied to your actual location
- search the product/store name in the USPTO database and obvious repo/package
  channels
- review [legal-checklist.md](legal-checklist.md)
  before first paid launch

## Tax and checkout reality

Stripe Checkout is the active checkout layer for new orders. It gives direct
control over checkout copy and routing, but it does not remove your own tax,
registration, and recordkeeping responsibilities as the seller.

## Identity and payout verification

No realistic launch stack skips verification entirely. Expect some combination
of identity verification, tax forms, payout-provider verification, and platform
review.

## Records to keep

- payouts received
- refunds issued
- order confirmations
- product versions delivered
- launch dates and price changes
