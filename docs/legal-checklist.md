# Legal Checklist

This is the practical non-lawyer checklist before taking public money.

This is not legal or tax advice.

## Seller identity

- Decide whether the seller name shown to buyers is your legal name or a brand.
- If the public-facing seller name is not your legal name, check whether Oregon
  requires an assumed business name filing for that use.
- Keep the decision written down in `docs/company-setup.md`.
- No LLC is planned for launch, so do not treat a brand label as automatically
  equivalent to a legal seller identity.

## Oregon and local checks

- Check Oregon Business Xpress.
- Check the Oregon license directory.
- Check whether your city or county adds any local requirement tied to your
  actual location.

## Tax and records

- Keep order confirmations.
- Keep payout records.
- Keep refund records.
- Keep price-change dates.
- Keep delivered version numbers for paid packs.

## Marketing and claims

- Do one truth-in-advertising pass on the site and launch posts.
- Do not imply full SQLAlchemy 2.0 migration coverage.
- Do not imply guaranteed passing application behavior.
- Keep testimonials and proof assets accurate and attributable.
- Keep [claims-safeguards.md](claims-safeguards.md)
  in sync with launch copy and product setup.

## Naming checks

- Search the USPTO database for obvious conflicts.
- Search GitHub, PyPI, and the open web for conflicting product names.
- If a conflict looks real, change the product label before leaning into it.
