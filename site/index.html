Boundaries

Presentation should feel serious because the limits are serious too.

What buyers are paying for

Less repetitive cleanup, clearer unsupported buckets, and a migration report that tells a team what happened.

The paid layer earns its keep on ugly edge cases, rollout confidence, and better docs for the people approving migration spend.

What this is not

It is not a full SQLAlchemy 2.0 migration robot. It does not fake support for transaction semantics, async migration, or full Query to select() rewrites.

If your repo fails validation after changes, that is a failed migration run, not a quiet success.

FAQ

Short answers for the normal skepticism.

Why not just use the migration guide?

You still should. This exists to remove the repetitive edits and leave the dangerous parts visible for deliberate follow-up by the repo owner.

How do I know whether it worked?

The free scan report shows supported candidates and unsupported findings. The paid apply pack adds validation results and the post-run migration report.

What if my repo is outside current coverage?

Use the free scan first. If the unsupported bucket is still faster to fix by hand, do not buy. If the unsupported patterns are the recurring expensive ones, that is where the paid layer helps.
