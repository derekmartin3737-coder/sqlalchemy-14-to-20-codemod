SQLAlchemy issue page

Retire joinedload_all(...) without pretending duplicate joins are a one-line rewrite.

Teams usually hit this during the SQLAlchemy 1.4 to 2.0 move when old eager-loading paths collide with manual join() calls, contains_eager(), aliases, or deeper path semantics. sa20-pack keeps that boundary explicit: automate the supported loader cleanup, and leave the risky query-shape cases visible for follow-up.

What teams are usually moving toward

Before
joinedload_all("children.school")
joinedload_all("children.teacher")

After
joinedload(Parent.children).joinedload(Child.school)
joinedload(Parent.children).joinedload(Child.teacher)

Do not buy based on this page alone

Queries that already mix manual join(), contains_eager(), alias-heavy loaders, or duplicate-join avoidance still need deliberate review. This storefront does not claim to auto-resolve broader eager-loading semantics just because joinedload_all(...) appears in the repo.
